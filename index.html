About Me

I am Atiyeh Nezhadebrahim, an interdisciplinary researcher at the intersection of neuroscience, biomechanics, and robotics. My work focuses on understanding how the nervous system controls movement — and translating that understanding into computational models.

With two master's degrees — one in Animal Biology (Physiology) and one in Mechanical Engineering — I bring a uniquely cross-disciplinary perspective to neurorobotics research. I have developed computational models of Central Pattern Generators, neuromusculoskeletal systems, and Parkinson's disease motor symptoms.

My research experience spans the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems in Stuttgart, Denmark Technical University in Copenhagen, and multiple research centers in Tehran. I am passionate about bridging biological motor control and intelligent robotic systems.
